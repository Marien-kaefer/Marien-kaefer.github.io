Image Analysis is supposed to be:

  1. Quantitative
    • Derive numbers from images which describe physical properties of the observed sample.

Image Analysis is supposed to be:

  1. Quantitative
  2. Objective
    • Derived measurement does not depend on who did the measurement, i.e., is free of interpretation.

Image Analysis is supposed to be:

  1. Quantitative
  2. Objective
  3. Reliable
    • Confidence, that the measurement is describing that it is supposed to describe.

Image Analysis is supposed to be:

  1. Quantitative
  2. Objective
  3. Reliable
  4. Reproducible
    • Somebody else can do the experiment under different conditions and gets similar measurements.

Image Analysis is supposed to be:

  1. Quantitative
  2. Objective
  3. Reliable
  4. Reproducible
  5. Repeatable
    • The experiment can be repeated twice under the same conditions and one gets similar results.
  • Multiple steps
  • Iterative process

Workflow documentation is critical for

  • reproducing results,
  • thesis/manuscript writing.

Multiple steps add up quickly:

  • Open file = 1 click
  • Analyse one file: Denoising, background subtraction, segmentation, labelling, feature extraction: ≥ 5 clicks + 1 → 6
  • Repeat for all images in condition: 10 × 6 → 60
  • Repeat for each condition: 3 × 60 → 180
  • ≥ 180 clicks per experiment!
